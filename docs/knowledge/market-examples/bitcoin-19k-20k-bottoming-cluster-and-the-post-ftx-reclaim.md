# Bitcoin’s $19k–$20k Bottoming Cluster and the Post-FTX Reclaim

## What this case shows
This case shows [confluence](../glossary/confluence.md) as a process, not a magic answer. Between June 2022 and January 2023, Bitcoin kept interacting with an area shaped by the old 2017 cycle high, the $20,000 zone, aggregate [holder cost basis](../glossary/holder-cost-basis.md) measures, and later long-term recovery markers. The area first looked relevant, then became worn down, then broke under FTX stress, and only later became more convincing again after better reclaims.

## What happened
After heavy 2022 damage, Bitcoin broke below $20,000 in June and traded into the high-$17,000s. On-chain work and market structure both started focusing attention on the $19,000 to $22,000 region. That area mattered for several reasons at once: the old 2017 high, the psychological $20,000 zone, [realized price](../glossary/realized-price.md) interaction, and later long-term holder and moving-average thresholds. Through summer and autumn, price kept moving back around the zone without resolving cleanly. That mattered because repeated crossings show a zone is active, but also that confidence is incomplete. Then FTX failed in November and price broke materially lower again. The old support thesis did not survive that shock. Later, however, the market rebuilt. In January 2023 Bitcoin reclaimed key cost-basis and trend markers again, making the recovery case stronger than the earlier summer attempts.

## Why it mattered
This is a strong confluence case because the useful evidence came from different families, not from duplicate indicators pretending to be a chorus. Historical structure, holder cost basis, realized losses, supply redistribution, and later volatility compression were each answering different questions. The case also teaches that real confluence can degrade. Agreement across evidence families can make an area more relevant without making it invulnerable.

## How PocketPilot would read it
PocketPilot would likely treat June and July 2022 as a high-stress bottom-discovery process, not a confirmed reversal. Strategy Status for Confluence Alignment could improve as evidence stacked up, but confidence should stay modest. Through August and October, the correct tone would likely shift from “strong estimated support cluster” to “still relevant, but degraded.” After FTX, the product should treat the break as thesis failure, not as a cosmetic overshoot. January 2023 is where the read becomes more constructive again, because the reclaim above important cost-basis and trend markers is more meaningful than earlier summer bounces.

## Strategy lens comparison
### Confluence Alignment
This is the clearest-fit lens. It benefits from independent evidence families pointing to the same broad area.

### Breakout Watcher / Trend Follow
These lenses would stay much more cautious during summer 2022 and only become more interested once January 2023 reclaimed stronger structural markers.

### Range Trader / Reversion Bounce
These lenses had more to work with during the repeated summer reactions around the zone, but the later FTX break showed why repeated tests were not proof of safety.

### Candle Signals
Useful as supporting texture, not as the backbone of the case.

## What a user could learn
Confluence is not “many indicators say buy.” It is different kinds of evidence pointing to the same place for different reasons. It can make a zone more important, but not automatically make it durable. Stronger evidence can improve the read without eliminating uncertainty.
