# Resistance

## What this means
A price area where selling tends to appear.

## Why it matters
It helps explain why upward moves can pause, stall, or reverse.

## How to think about it
Treat resistance as a zone that can weaken over repeated tests.

## Common misunderstanding
Assuming resistance cannot be broken.

## Layer 2: Deeper understanding

## How to recognise this
Resistance is an area where upward movement slows, pauses, or meets repeated selling pressure.

It often shows up when price reaches a similar area more than once and struggles to continue higher. Like support, resistance is better understood as a zone rather than an exact line.

The key sign is that buyers are no longer moving price higher as easily in that area.

## How it usually develops
Resistance often forms after price reaches an area where selling previously appeared, buyers slow down, or movement becomes more balanced.

It can become more meaningful after repeated failed attempts to move higher. It can also weaken if price keeps pressing into the same area and selling pressure becomes less effective.

If price eventually moves beyond resistance, the behaviour after the break matters more than the break alone.

## What it is commonly confused with
- a single high point on a chart
- a random pause after upward movement
- an exact price ceiling
- a level that must always reject price

## What makes it clearer
Resistance is easier to interpret when price reacts to the area more than once.

Helpful signs include:
- repeated rejection near a similar zone
- slowing upward movement near the area
- visible pressure building below the level
- alignment with broader range, breakout, or trend context

## What makes it less reliable
Resistance becomes less reliable when reactions become inconsistent or pressure keeps building through the area.

Noisier signs include:
- sharp moves both above and below the level
- weak or inconsistent rejection
- strong follow-through beyond the area
- little prior interaction with the zone

## How it appears in examples
Resistance appears in examples where price repeatedly struggles near the upper boundary. It also appears in breakout examples where price moves beyond the old ceiling, and in caution examples where the breakout attempt fails and price returns below it.
