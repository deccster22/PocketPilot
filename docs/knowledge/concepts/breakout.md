# Breakout

## What this means
Price moving beyond a key level.

## Why it matters
It highlights moments when market structure may be changing.

## How to think about it
Read breakouts with confirmation context, not as instant certainty.

## Common misunderstanding
Assuming every breakout continues cleanly.

## Layer 2: Deeper understanding

## How to recognise this
A breakout appears when price moves beyond a level or area that had previously contained it.

The level may be a resistance area above price, a support area below price, or the edge of a range. The important behaviour is not just crossing the level. It is whether price begins to behave differently after crossing it.

A breakout can be sharp and obvious, or it can develop more slowly after repeated pressure against the same area.

## How it usually develops
Breakouts often develop after compression, repeated tests, or a build-up of pressure near a boundary. Price may approach the same level several times before moving through it.

After the break, the next phase is often the most informative. Price may continue away from the level, pause near it, or return back inside the prior range.

That follow-through helps distinguish a developing breakout from a brief move through a boundary.

## What it is commonly confused with
- any fast move through a price level
- a temporary spike beyond a boundary
- noise around support or resistance
- momentum, which may occur without a meaningful level being broken

## What makes it clearer
Breakout behaviour is easier to interpret when the level was already meaningful before the move.

Helpful signs include:
- repeated interaction with the same area
- tighter movement before the break
- follow-through after the initial move
- price holding outside the prior range for more than a brief moment

## What makes it less reliable
Breakouts become harder to read when the surrounding behaviour is messy.

Noisier signs include:
- wide swings through and back across the level
- immediate rejection after the break
- little or no follow-through
- a level that was not clearly meaningful beforehand

## How it appears in examples
Breakout appears in examples where price moves beyond an old boundary and then continues to behave differently. It also appears in caution examples where price briefly escapes a level, then snaps back into the previous range.
