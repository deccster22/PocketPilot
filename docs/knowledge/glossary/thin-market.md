# thin market

## Plain-English meaning
A thin market is a market with too little real depth to absorb activity cleanly.

## Canonical definition
In PocketPilot, a thin market refers to conditions where order-book depth, liquidity, or participation are weak enough that price can jump around more than usual.

## Why it matters
Thin markets can make signals look louder and execution worse than they would in healthier conditions.
