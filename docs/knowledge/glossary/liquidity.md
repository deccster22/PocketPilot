# liquidity

## Plain-English meaning
Liquidity is how easily something can be bought, sold, or moved without causing a big disruption.

## Canonical definition
In PocketPilot, liquidity refers to how much real trading depth and market capacity exists around an asset or venue. Higher liquidity usually means smoother entries, exits, and price discovery. Lower liquidity usually means more slippage, fragility, and surprise.

## Why it matters
Liquidity changes what strategies can reasonably do. A market can look attractive on the chart while still being awkward, fragile, or dangerous to trade if liquidity is weak.
