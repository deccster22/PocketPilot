# From Liquidity Tailwind to Deleveraging Grind

## What this case shows
This case shows a broad phase change rather than one dramatic day. Crypto moved from a liquidity-rich, momentum-friendly environment into a tightening-and-[deleveraging](../glossary/deleveraging.md) [regime](../glossary/regime.md) where the old playbook stopped fitting as well. The important teaching value is not the list of disasters by itself. It is how strategy comfort changed as the backdrop changed.

## What happened
The earlier phase was still expansionary. In late 2021, Bitcoin and the wider crypto market were still printing record highs. Then the backdrop began to shift. Taper acceleration, rising rates, weaker risk appetite, and tighter liquidity changed the macro environment. Early 2022 was messy rather than clean. January damage was real, but some long-term holders still looked constructive and the market still managed a March breakout attempt. Later, stacked breaks turned the picture clearer. Terra failed in May. Celsius froze withdrawals in June. Three Arrows Capital unraveled. Policy stayed tight. Then FTX failed in November. By year end the market was operating in a much less forgiving environment than the one that had helped earlier expansion.

## Why it mattered
This is a strong macro-cycle-transition case because it teaches that phase change often arrives in layers. Local recoveries can still appear while the broader environment is deteriorating. That is why the same chart pattern can mean something different in a different phase. In an expansionary regime, dips and breakouts may have wind behind them. In a deleveraging regime, the same shapes may be much more fragile.

## How PocketPilot would read it
PocketPilot would likely treat the transition as a progression in confidence, not as a prophecy. The 30,000 ft View might move from trend-friendly expansion to transitioning or mixed confidence, then later toward confirmed deleveraging and reduced [strategy fit](../glossary/strategy-fit.md). Snapshot in late March could still look constructive because price briefly improved. Dashboard should remain more careful because the broader support conditions had already weakened. By May and June, macro pressure and crypto-native stress were aligning in the same direction, making [meaningful change](../glossary/meaningful-change.md) a much stronger read.

## Strategy lens comparison
### Trend Follow
Comfortable in the earlier expansionary phase, then more defensive as structural damage and macro pressure increased.

### Buy the Dip
Still usable in places, but far less naturally rewarded once the background stopped being supportive.

### Range Trader
More relevant during the ambiguous transition, then much less comfortable once the range itself gave way.

### Breakout Watcher
Upside breaks looked more natural in the earlier phase. Later, downside breaks carried more weight because context had changed.

### Reversion Bounce
Still had tactical windows, but now against a much more damaged backdrop.

### Confluence Alignment
Especially useful here because it respects altitude. As more independent pressures lined up, the regime read became stronger.

## What a user could learn
The lesson is not “you should have known.” The lesson is that phase change is often visible before it is universally acknowledged, but usually not in a neat cinematic way. Strategy comfort is contextual. The same market can be a different market when the broader environment changes.
