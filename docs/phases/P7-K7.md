# P7-K7 - Knowledge Topic Detail Context Framing

## Why This Phase Happened Now

P7-K4 established the calm live knowledge lane on Dashboard and Trade Hub.
P7-K5 kept that lane subordinate and density-aware.
P7-K6 improved the live linkage quality so the same prepared lane could choose better topics from strategy, signal, event, and surface context.

P7-K7 exists because the next honest step is not a broader knowledge surface or a new recommendation layer. It is to preserve the same calm context when a user opens a topic detail view from Dashboard or Trade Hub, so the handoff feels continuous without becoming a gate, inbox, or advice system.

## What Was Improved

- Added one service-owned topic-detail context-framing seam in `services/knowledge/createKnowledgeTopicContextFraming.ts`.
- Added canonical origin and framing types so the topic-detail handoff stays explicit and deterministic.
- Threaded the prepared frame through topic-detail build and fetch paths.
- Let Dashboard and Trade Hub pass prepared contextual origin metadata into topic detail.
- Kept the app passive by rendering only prepared values.

## What Was Deliberately Not Added

- No execution dispatch.
- No gating or lockout behavior.
- No recommendation feed.
- No inbox, push, or background monitoring mechanics.
- No forced reading flow or mandatory tutorial.
- No wholesale redesign of Knowledge Library, Dashboard, or Trade Hub.
- No UI-side framing logic.

## What Future P7 Work Can Build On

- Richer but still calm topic-detail copy when later phases author more detailed contextual reasons.
- Additional prepared explanation seams only if the product explicitly needs them.
- Further polish on the continuity between the live shelf and topic detail without changing ownership of selection or framing.

## Recommendation Handling

- Adopt now: keep the service-owned framing seam and the passive app rendering path.
- Add to backlog: any broader contextual variants, richer explanation text, or additional surfaces beyond Dashboard and Trade Hub.
- Decline for now: gating, advice, recommendation-feed behavior, inbox mechanics, push notifications, and background work.
