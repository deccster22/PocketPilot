# Support, Resistance, and Structure

## What this means
[Support](../glossary/support.md) and [resistance](../glossary/resistance.md) describe areas where price behavior has repeatedly mattered. Structure is the broader shape formed by those interactions, such as a [range](../glossary/range.md), a ceiling, a floor, or a transition zone.

## Why it matters
Many strategies depend on whether a level still matters, whether it is becoming weaker, or whether the market is starting to behave differently around it.

## What PocketPilot is trying to help you see
PocketPilot uses structure to explain why a [breakout](../glossary/breakout.md) is interesting, why [rejection](../glossary/rejection.md) matters, and why repeated tests can strengthen or degrade a zone over time.

## Common mistake
A common mistake is treating levels like magical lines instead of areas where the market is negotiating.
