# Support

## What this means
A price area where buying tends to appear.

## Why it matters
It helps explain why some declines slow, pause, or bounce.

## How to think about it
Treat support as a zone that can strengthen or fail, not as a perfect line.

## Common misunderstanding
Assuming support will always hold.

## Layer 2: Deeper understanding

## How to recognise this
Support is an area where downward movement slows, pauses, or meets repeated buying interest.

It is usually easier to see after price reacts to a similar area more than once. Support is best understood as a zone, not a single perfect line. Price may move slightly through or around it before the behaviour becomes clear.

The key sign is that sellers are no longer moving price lower as easily in that area.

## How it usually develops
Support often forms after price reaches an area where buyers previously responded, sellers slow down, or market behaviour becomes more balanced.

It can strengthen when price reacts to the area multiple times. It can weaken when price keeps returning to the same area with less bounce each time.

If support breaks clearly, the area may later become useful context in a different way, but it should not be treated as reliable just because it mattered before.

## What it is commonly confused with
- a single low point on a chart
- an arbitrary horizontal line
- any area where price bounced once
- a guaranteed floor

## What makes it clearer
Support is easier to interpret when reactions around the area repeat.

Helpful signs include:
- several reactions near a similar price area
- slowing downward movement near the zone
- stronger response after price reaches the area
- alignment with broader strategy context, such as range behaviour or pullbacks

## What makes it less reliable
Support becomes less reliable when price keeps returning to the area with weaker reactions.

Noisier signs include:
- fast movement straight through the zone
- repeated tests with little response
- large volatility around the area
- no clear prior behaviour at that level

## How it appears in examples
Support appears in examples where price stabilises near a repeated area, including ranges and pullbacks. It also appears in caution examples where the same area fails to hold and price begins behaving as if the old floor no longer matters.
