# execution risk

## Plain-English meaning
Execution risk is the risk that even if your market read is right, actually getting in or out may go badly.

## Canonical definition
In PocketPilot, execution risk refers to the danger created by poor liquidity, venue issues, fast movement, congestion, halted rails, or fragmented price discovery. It is about how hard the trade or action is to express cleanly in the real market.

## Why it matters
A correct direction does not guarantee a usable outcome. PocketPilot uses execution risk to stop price-only interpretation from pretending the market is cleaner than it is.
