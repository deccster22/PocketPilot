# Terra: From Peg Wobble to Exit Spiral

## What this case shows
This case shows how a market can move from stressed but still interpretable conditions into deeper structural impairment. The Terra collapse was not just a big red candle. It was a progression: peg wobble, failed stabilization, volatility expansion, venue stress, and then outright trust-and-execution breakdown.

## What happened
Before the break, Terra already had visible fragility beneath the surface. Anchor had grown huge, subsidies were under strain, and the broader market was already risk-off. In early May 2022, large withdrawals, shallow liquidity, and mounting concern around UST triggered a [depeg](../glossary/depeg.md) sequence. The stabilization mechanism did not restore durable confidence. UST moved materially away from the dollar, arbitrage flow diluted LUNA, and the system deteriorated rapidly. Then conditions worsened further: exchange withdrawals slowed, reserves were deployed under stress, other stablecoins showed strain, and finally the chain itself halted after severe LUNA inflation created governance risk.

## Why it mattered
This case is useful because it teaches that volatility is not just speed. It can also be a change in what information deserves weight. Earlier in Terra’s life, smaller peg deviations had repaired. During the May 2022 collapse, that old playbook stopped fitting. The event became a broader trust shock for crypto in 2022 and helped mark a new, harsher local [regime](../glossary/regime.md).

## How PocketPilot would read it
PocketPilot would likely read this as a progression:
- estimated stress
- [meaningful change](../glossary/meaningful-change.md)
- structural impairment

The relevant MarketEvents were not just “UST down” or “LUNA down.” They included accelerating withdrawals, peg failure that did not normalize cleanly, emergency reserve deployment, exchange congestion, contagion into other stablecoins, and eventually a chain halt. Strategy Status would likely move from mixed and watchful to reduced [strategy fit](../glossary/strategy-fit.md) for reversion or ranging, and later toward execution-impaired conditions. The 30,000 ft View would also matter, because the event was hitting inside a broader macro risk-off environment.

## Strategy lens comparison
### Trend Follow
Downside continuation became more legible as the depeg stopped mean-reverting cleanly and stress persisted.

### Buy the Dip
This lens became much more fragile as old repair assumptions stopped fitting the environment.

### Range Trader
Earlier peg-normalization behavior had once made range logic plausible. That became far less comfortable once the system stopped defending the same boundaries.

### Breakout Watcher
This lens became more useful during the transition from wobble to break, then less useful once execution conditions themselves deteriorated.

### Reversion Bounce
This became the most tempting and the most dangerous read. Brief rebounds existed, but their foundation kept weakening.

### Confluence Alignment
This lens is especially useful here because price, liquidity, large-wallet behavior, reserve confidence, and infrastructure quality all moved in the same direction over time.

## What a user could learn
The lesson is not “never buy weakness” or “everything is always doomed.” It is that volatility can change context faster than narrative. The same kind of price move can be a bounce setup in one regime and a trap in another. More drama does not automatically create more clarity.
