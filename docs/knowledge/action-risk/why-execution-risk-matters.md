# Why Execution Risk Matters

## What this means
[Execution risk](../glossary/execution-risk.md) is the risk that even if your market read is directionally right, actually getting in or out may go badly.

## Why it matters
A clean chart can hide ugly reality. Poor [liquidity](../glossary/liquidity.md), unstable venues, violent [volatility](../glossary/volatility.md), or deeper [structural impairment](../glossary/structural-impairment.md) can make action much worse than it looks.

## How PocketPilot uses it
PocketPilot should not pretend that price direction is the whole story. It should help users understand when action quality has degraded.

## Common mistake
A common mistake is assuming that being right about the market automatically means the action will work cleanly.
